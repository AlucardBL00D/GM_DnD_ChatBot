# D&D 5e Mechanics System

## Combat Resolution

### Initiative
* Roll 1d20 + DEX modifier
* Higher rolls act first
* Initiative order persists for the entire combat

### Attack Rolls
* Roll 1d20 + Attack Modifier
* Compare to target's Armor Class (AC)
* 20+ AC = Hit
* Roll twice on 20, roll once on 1 (critical hit/fail)

### Damage Calculation
* Roll damage dice based on weapon/spell
* Add ability modifier (STR for melee, DEX for ranged, etc)
* Apply resistances or vulnerabilities
* Reduce target's HP

### Action Economy
Each turn, a creature can:
* Take 1 Action (Attack, Cast Spell, Dodge, etc)
* Take 1 Bonus Action (if class feature allows)
* Take 1 Reaction (if opportunity arises)
* Move up to their speed

## Ability Checks

### DC (Difficulty Class)
* Easy: DC 10
* Medium: DC 15
* Hard: DC 20
* Nearly Impossible: DC 25+

### Rolling for Checks
* Roll 1d20 + relevant ability modifier
* Compare to DC
* Roll 20 or higher = Success

### Common Checks
* Stealth (DEX)
* Perception (WIS)
* Investigation (INT)
* Insight (WIS)
* Persuasion (CHA)
* Deception (CHA)
* Intimidation (CHA)
* Arcana (INT)

---

## Saving Throws

### When to Roll
* When resisting magic effects
* When avoiding hazards
* When resisting poison, disease, etc

### Save DCs
* Magic DC = 8 + caster's ability modifier + proficiency bonus
* Hazard DC = depends on the hazard

### Ability Save Connections
* STR Save (resisting grapple, shoves)
* DEX Save (dodging traps, area effects)
* CON Save (resisting poison, magic)
* INT Save (resisting mind control)
* WIS Save (resisting illusions, fear)
* CHA Save (resisting charm effects)

---

## Death Saves

### When Active
* When a creature is reduced to 0 HP
* 3 failures = Death
* 3 successes = Stabilize

### Rolling
* Roll 1d20 (no modifiers)
* 10+ = Success
* 9 or lower = Failure
* 20 = Automatic success + regain 1 HP
* 1 = Automatic failure (counts as 2)

---

## Conditions

### Common Conditions
* **Blinded**: Can't see, auto-miss
* **Charmed**: Disadvantage vs charm magic, adverse actions
* **Deafened**: Can't hear
* **Frightened**: Disadvantage on checks/saves, can't move toward frightener
* **Grappled**: Speed becomes 0, can't benefit from speed boost
* **Paralyzed**: Can't move or speak
* **Petrified**: Turned to stone, immune to poison/disease
* **Poisoned**: Disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks
* **Prone**: Disadvantage on melee attacks, easier to hit
* **Restrained**: Speed 0, disadvantage on DEX saves/attacks
* **Stunned**: Can't move or speak, fail STR/DEX saves, attack rolls vs = advantage

---

## Advantage and Disadvantage

### Advantage
Roll twice, use the higher result.

Causes:
* Many class features and spells
* High ground in combat
* Targeting unaware enemy

### Disadvantage
Roll twice, use the lower result.

Causes:
* Attacking while prone
* Attacking with disadvantage from spells
* Blinded or obscured vision

---

## Skill Proficiency

When proficient in a skill:
* Add proficiency bonus (usually +2 to +6)
* Proficiency only applies if relevant ability modifier would apply

### Proficiency by Class
* Different classes have different skill proficiencies
* Always consult the rule book when unsure
