# NPC & World Behavior System

## NPC Creation Principles

### Every NPC Must Have
* **Name and appearance** - Memorable and descriptive
* **Personality traits** - At least 2-3 defining characteristics
* **Motivations** - What drives their actions
* **Relationships** - How they relate to other NPCs and world events
* **Voice** - Unique speaking style or dialect
* **Fears and desires** - What they want and what they avoid

### NPC Consistency
* NPCs must act according to their motivations
* Personality remains consistent across sessions
* NPCs remember past interactions with the player
* Relationships can evolve based on player choices
* Death or major events affect other NPCs

---

## Dialogue Guidelines

### Natural Conversation
* Avoid generic responses
* Use NPC personality quirks (hesitations, humor, accent, etc)
* Let NPCs challenge the player
* NPCs should ask questions back
* Avoid monologuing unless character-appropriate

### Example Good Dialogue
```
"Look, I've seen adventurers come and go. You lot usually end up 
as buzzard food. But I'm desperate. Take this job, and I'll pay you 
what I promised. Fail me, and you'll understand why merchants don't 
cross the Crimson Hand."
```

### Example Bad Dialogue
```
"Hello adventurer. I need your help. Will you help me?"
```

## World Reactions

### The World Responds
Every player action has consequences:
* **Combat**: NPCs nearby are alerted, reinforcements may come
* **Theft**: Guards investigate, reputation suffers
* **Kindness**: NPCs remember, offer better deals or information
* **Betrayal**: NPCs become enemies, allies withdraw support
* **Destruction**: Property damage has costs, owners seek compensation

### Rumor & Reputation
* NPCs talk to each other
* Actions spread as rumors (possibly exaggerated)
* Player reputation affects NPC reactions
* Good deeds earn trust, crimes earn wanted posters
* Fame can precede the player

## Quest Giving

### Effective Quests
* **Personal stakes** - Why does THIS NPC need help?
* **Multiple solutions** - Don't railroad the approach
* **Consequences** - Completing or failing has world impact
* **Negotiable terms** - Let player propose different rewards
* **Hints, not solutions** - Provide information, not directions

### Quest Structure
1. **Hook** - Why the player should care
2. **Objective** - What needs to be done
3. **Stakes** - What happens if they succeed/fail
4. **Flexibility** - How many ways can this be solved?

## Social Encounters

### Persuasion, Deception, Intimidation
* Roll Charisma checks when outcome is uncertain
* NPC personality affects DC
* Don't let a single roll resolve everything
* Multiple checks for complex negotiations

### Skill Challenges
* A single check might not be enough
* "You need 2 successes before 1 failure" structure
* Failures create complications, not automatic stops
* Creative solutions can bypass checks entirely

---

## Moral Complexity

### Avoid Black & White
* NPCs have justifiable motivations
* Enemies might have good reasons
* Allies might have hidden agendas
* Moral choices should be genuinely difficult

### Ethical Dilemmas
* Present situations where all choices have costs
* Let player choose their moral path
* Don't punish for roleplaying their character
* Consequences should be logical, not punishment

---

## NPC Evolution

### Tracking Changes
* Update NPC status after major events
* NPCs grow relationships with the party
* Enemies become allies or vice versa
* NPCs pursue their own goals between sessions
* Death of important NPCs affects the world

### Long-term Story
* NPCs remember everything the player does
* Past acquaintances may reappear
* Former enemies might seek revenge
* Allies might ask for favors
* The world continues to change

---

## Conflict Resolution (Non-Combat)

### When Things Get Tense
* Allow player agency in conflict
* Present options (fight, flee, negotiate, trick)
* Combat isn't the only solution
* Consequences exist for all choices
* Relationships can be repaired or damaged

---

## Creating Memorable Encounters

### What Makes an Encounter Memorable
* Unique NPC personality
* Unexpected reactions
* Moral dilemmas
* Humor (when appropriate)
* Emotional stakes
* Consequences the player cares about

### Avoid Forgettable NPCs
* Generic descriptions
* Robotic dialogue
* No personal motivations
* Forgettable names
* No ongoing relationships
